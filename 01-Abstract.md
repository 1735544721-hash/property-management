Abstract

In residential property management, resident repair requests, fee payments, announcements, and complaint handling are scattered across different systems or offline processes, and the resulting data silos and non-traceable statuses lead to slow work-order responses, difficult fee reconciliation, and opaque service evaluation; meanwhile, multi-role collaboration among residents, property staff, and managers lacks unified permissions and a closed data loop, weakening community governance and service efficiency. This study designs and implements a front-end/back-end separated information system for residential property management: the front end uses Vue 3 and Element Plus for resident and admin interfaces, and the back end is built on Spring Boot 3.2, MyBatis-Plus, Spring Security, JWT, and MySQL, covering users and permissions, buildings and houses, repair work orders, fee management, announcements, complaint handling, and dashboards, and it improves service response and fee traceability through workflow transitions, reminder mechanisms, and statistical analysis, providing reliable data support for community governance and operational decisions.

Keywords: residential property management; repair work orders; fee management; Spring Boot
